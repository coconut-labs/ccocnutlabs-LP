Coconut Labs is a small inference research lab. We work on schedulers, systems notes, and the small pieces of infrastructure that decide whether shared compute behaves like a tool or a tax.

The lab is intentionally small: two engineers, close to the measurements. A result should have a harness. A claim should have a number. A page should leave enough quiet around the thing it is trying to say.

The first public thread is KVWarden: tenant fairness on shared inference. mlxd follows the same line closer to local Apple Silicon inference. Both projects are about making the shared layer more honest under pressure.
